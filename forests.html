What Is Deforestation

Forests are being cleared at a pace that the natural world cannot recover from. Deforestation happens when large areas of trees are removed for farming, building, mining or logging. Once these trees are cut down, the land loses the ability to support many species and the soil becomes weak and dry. Forests that took centuries to grow can disappear in a matter of weeks, leaving long lasting damage behind.

Why Forests Matter

Forests play a vital role in keeping the planet healthy. They regulate temperature by storing carbon, which slows down climate change. They protect soil from erosion and help to prevent flooding. They are also home to over half of the worldâ€™s land based species. Many communities depend on forests for food, shelter and traditional livelihoods. When forests are destroyed, these natural systems break down and the effects can be felt across the world.

The Main Causes of Forest Loss

The biggest driver of forest loss today is agriculture. Land is often cleared for cattle farming, soy plantations and palm oil production. Logging also removes huge numbers of trees for construction and furniture. Roads, mining sites and expanding cities push deeper into natural areas each year. In some regions, rising temperatures and drought have caused more frequent wildfires, which quickly destroy habitats and are difficult to control.

Protecting existing forests is one of the most effective ways to support life on land and reduce climate damage. Small actions from individuals and communities make a real difference.
